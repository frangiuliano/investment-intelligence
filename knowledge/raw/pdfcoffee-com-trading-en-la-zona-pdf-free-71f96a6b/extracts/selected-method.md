- Signals / checks:
  - Treat every market outcome as uncertain; analysis raises odds, never certainty.
  - Prefer probabilistic framing for research hypotheses / stance: edge over a *series* of similar setups, not “this one must work.”
  - Each similar setup is statistically independent of the last (casino / sample thinking) — do not rewrite the method after one win or loss.
  - Predefine invalidation before committing to a stance; keep criteria objective (present vs absent).
  - Do not let the last 2–3 outcomes dominate perceived risk of the next read.
  - Watch for revenge / “prove the market wrong” framing after a near miss — bias filter on evidence.
  - Pattern resemblance is superficial: same chart template, different participants → uncertain path.
  - Accepting uncertainty reduces threat-filtering of contradictory information (better objectivity).
- Materiality hints (signal → low|medium|high):
  - Demanding more analysis to eliminate uncertainty → low (process smell)
  - Single confirming outcome as proof the method is fixed → low
  - Emotional narrative / revenge around a position → low for alert quality; note in risks
  - Clear predefined invalidation on a thesis → supports research quality (not a materiality upgrade alone)
- Invalidation / failure modes:
  - Believing identical-looking patterns must resolve the same way.
  - Skipping predefined invalidation “just this once.”
  - Changing rules mid-sample after a few outcomes.
  - Expecting the market to fulfill personal hopes; markets owe the operator nothing.
  - Blocking contradictory information because it threatens an expectation.
  - Overconfidence after a streak of confirming outcomes.
- Anti-patterns (do not):
  - Do not frame psychology as broker buy/sell orders.
  - Do not claim certainty from more chart study alone.
  - Do not invent stops as executed orders — map to research invalidation / horizon only.
  - Do not treat courage/nerves as substitutes for predefined risk boundaries.
- Quotes or paraphrases worth keeping (short):
  - Anything can happen / unknown forces always operate.
  - Edge shows over a series; each hand/trade is independent.
  - Objectivity = access to learned method without pain-avoidance filters.
  - Professionals’ risk perception is not driven by the last few outcomes.
