Book is Saifedean Ammous–style monetary theory (*El patrón Bitcoin*), **not**
chart-pattern TA. Paraphrased research method for equity/macro briefs —
discard ideology-as-certainty and invented on-chain stats.

- Signals / checks:
  - Distinguish **money / store of value** framing from **investment** framing:
    money aims to hold purchasing power with lower intentional risk; investment
    expects return for bearing loss risk — do not collapse them in a brief.
  - When a source argues “hard” vs “easy” money: check claimed scarcity
    mechanism (stock vs new flow / issuance policy) — do not invent stock-to-flow
    ratios or supply numbers absent from tools/sources.
  - Fiat settlement between institutions can imply **counterparty** and FX risk
    during delayed clearing — relevant only when the article’s catalyst is
    payments/settlement, not as a generic BTC pitch.
  - Easy-money / CB expansion narratives may raise time-preference and
    debt-financed behavior in macro color — keep as hypothesis, not forecast.
  - For Bitcoin-specific claims: focus on **monetary properties** argued
    (issuance schedule, settlement finality, third-party risk) rather than
    chemistry-like protocol trivia unless needed for the catalyst.
  - Gold vs fiat vs BTC comparisons are narrative lenses; require primary facts
    for any numeric claim.
- Materiality hints (signal → low|medium|high):
  - Pure monetary philosophy / anti-Keynes essay with no ticker/catalyst → low
  - Generic “print money / hard money” op-ed → low (digest)
  - Policy/CB action tied to held FX or rate-sensitive names → medium–high via
    existing macro heuristics
  - Bitcoin network/policy/issuance news with watchlist/holding crypto exposure →
    use company/event heuristics if a ticker exists; jargon alone ≠ high
  - Store-of-value certainty language (“must appreciate”) → low (process smell)
- Invalidation / failure modes:
  - Treating ideology as verified market microstructure.
  - Inventing scarcity metrics or historical “proof” without sources.
  - Upgrading equity alerts solely because a hard-money narrative appeared.
  - Confusing money demand / time-preference essays with earnings catalysts.
- Anti-patterns (do not):
  - Do not turn the book into buy-BTC product orders.
  - Do not invent on-chain volumes, hashrate, or S2F forecasts.
  - Do not present Austrian cycle stories as certain predictions.
- Quotes or paraphrases worth keeping (short):
  - Hard money ≈ costly to inflate supply relative to stock; easy money ≈ elastic.
  - Investment rewards risk; money-as-SoV is a different job than a risk asset.
  - Settlement delay can leave counterparties exposed — context, not a trade.
