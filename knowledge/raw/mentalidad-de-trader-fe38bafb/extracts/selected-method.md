### Extract: mentalidad-de-trader (Fin-ranked selected chunks)

Ranking: `npm run knowledge:rank-chunks -- … --genre trading_psychology`
using `knowledge/_prompts/filter-themes.json` v1.1.0 (Finance Advisor).
Selected 2 / 3 chunks (`selected-chunks.json`). Paraphrased **research method**
only — discarded motivational / sales / faith fluff.

- Signals / checks:
  - Accept risk as the price of seeking research benefit; fear of loss and
    fleeing uncertainty can block acting on a prepared thesis.
  - Risk only capital you can afford to lose; predefine risk before committing
    to a stance (map stops → invalidation / horizon, never broker orders).
  - Judge whether expected benefit justifies the risk for this review cycle.
  - Prefer expectancy over a *series* of similar setups (low win rate can still
    be fine if edge holds); audit outcomes honestly.
  - After the thesis is set: avoid babysitting charts; do not rewrite evidence
    from discomfort.
  - Respect session/context: do not force reads in dead conditions; use downtime
    to study/plan.
  - Stay student-mode: no absolute truth.
- Materiality hints (signal → low|medium|high):
  - Motivational / promo / destiny framing → low
  - Fear/FOMO/revenge with no company event → low
  - Predefined risk + invalidation present → process quality (not alone a
    materiality upgrade)
- Invalidation / failure modes:
  - No predefined risk boundary before leaning on a stance.
  - Rules changed mid-sample after a few outcomes.
  - Fear of past failures filters contradictory evidence.
  - Micromanaging the thesis because watching the chart feels safer.
- Anti-patterns (do not):
  - Do not ingest MetaTrader / lotaje / “comprá ahora” recipes as product orders.
  - Do not equate confidence or courage with guaranteed outcomes.
  - Do not keep faith/motivational slogans in injectable bullets.
- Quotes or paraphrases worth keeping (short):
  - Benefit requires risk; fear of losing blocks participation.
  - Measure expectancy; honesty about wins/losses beats vibes.
  - Unwilling to lose → unprepared to benefit.
