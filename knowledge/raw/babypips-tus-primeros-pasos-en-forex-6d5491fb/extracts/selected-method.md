- Signals / checks:
  - Candles encode OHLC: long real body → strong buy/sell pressure; short body →
    quiet session; long shadows → extremes tested; spinning tops (small body +
    both shadows) → indecision.
  - Marubozu (no shadows): session fully controlled by buyers (empty/white) or
    sellers (filled/black) — continuation *or* reversal context still needed.
  - Doji (open ≈ close): struggle/tie; after a long body, often exhaustion cue —
    **require confirming close** beyond the prior long candle’s open before
    treating as reversal.
  - Reversal patterns need a **prior trend** (bullish reversal after downtrend;
    bearish after uptrend). Same silhouette ≠ same meaning: hammer (after fall)
    vs hanging man (after rise); inverted hammer (after fall) vs shooting star
    (after rise). Shadow ~2–3× body; little/no opposite shadow.
  - Confirmation habit: wait for a close past the open of the pattern’s left /
    reference candle — pattern alone is not enough.
  - S/R forms from successive swing highs/lows; treat levels as **zones**, not
    exact prints. Wicks often *test* a zone without a true break; closes through
    a level can still fail (false break). Line charts (closes only) help define
    zones with less wick noise.
  - After a break, a short pullback into former resistance (now support) can
    confirm the new direction — still not a guaranteed continuation.
  - Fibonacci retracements (0.236, 0.382, 0.500, 0.618, 0.764) and expansions /
    extensions (≈0.382–1.618) are **crowd-expectation** S/R or objective zones —
    useful with other structure, weak alone. 0.236 often weakest; markets may
    ignore all Fib levels. Swing high/low placement is subjective (common rule:
    pivot bar with two lower highs / higher lows on each side).
  - Moving averages smooth price and help state trend; MACD (MA of MAs +
    histogram) flags early trend change but **lags**; Parabolic SAR / RSI /
    stochastic appear as toolbox items — prefer confluence over single-tool
    stories.
  - Bollinger: bands as mini S/R; **bounce** (mean reversion to mid) better in
    ranges; **squeeze** (narrow bands) → watch for breakout risk — direction
    needs confirmation after which side breaks.
  - Pivot points (prior-session derived): better after trend direction is stated;
    often more useful in ranges (oscillation R1–S1); news/volatility can blow
    through R2/S2/R3/S3. Prefer pivots + candle/MACD/MA/stoch confluence.
  - Multi-timeframe: higher TF structure dominates; LTF “bullish” setups that tag
    HTF resistance/channel top often fail — establish HTF first.
  - Process: combine a few indicators that agree; there is no magic combo —
    know how each reacts to price. Match review cadence to horizon (swing vs
    day) without FOMO to always be in the market.
- Materiality hints (signal → low|medium|high):
  - Headline only names Fib / pivot / doji / hammer / MACD → low
  - Indicator or pattern jargon as sole upgrade to an alert → low
  - Verified multi-TF S/R or Fib zone + structure + company catalyst → low–medium
  - Certainty that a Fib level or candle pattern “must” reverse → low
- Invalidation / failure modes:
  - Treating wick pierce or one close through S/R as a definitive break.
  - Fib or pivot used alone; subjective swings redrawn post-hoc so levels never
    fail.
  - Reversal candle without prior trend or without confirming close.
  - Range tools (Bollinger bounce, pivots R1–S1) applied in a clear strong trend
    without stating regime.
  - LTF thesis that ignores opposing HTF structure.
  - Indicator lag (e.g. MACD) treated as early certainty.
- Anti-patterns (do not):
  - Do not map “buy lower band / sell upper band / enter at Fib / stop at swing”
    into product orders.
  - Do not invent pip targets, lot sizes, or broker setup as research method.
  - Do not promote pattern/indicator names to `event_type`.
  - Do not treat TOC / broker-comparison chapters as method.
- Quotes or paraphrases worth keeping (short):
  - S/R are zones; wicks often test, not break.
  - Fib levels alone will not make you rich — integrate with other analysis.
  - Higher timeframe makes support/resistance more important.
  - No magic indicator combination; study how each reacts to price.
